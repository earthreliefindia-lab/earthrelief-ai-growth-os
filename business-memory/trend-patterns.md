# Trend Patterns Library

This file teaches Earth Relief agents how to convert social trends into business-focused sustainability content.

## Pattern 1: Hero Defeats Villain

Original structure:

- Hero faces danger
- Villain threatens something valuable
- Hero wins
- Audience feels victory and relief

Earth Relief conversion:

- Hero = responsible business / Earth / sustainable packaging
- Villain = plastic pollution / unsafe waste / outdated packaging habits
- Ending = business chooses sustainable packaging and protects brand image

Example CTA:

Switch to Earth Relief biodegradable packaging before pollution wins.

## Pattern 2: POV Meme

Original structure:

- Relatable situation
- Humor or surprise
- Fast punchline

Earth Relief conversion:

- Business owner ignores plastic problem
- Customer notices poor packaging choice
- Brand perception drops

Example CTA:

Your packaging speaks before your product does.

## Pattern 3: Before vs After

Original structure:

- Bad current state
- Better future state
- Visual contrast

Earth Relief conversion:

- Before = plastic-heavy packaging and low brand trust
- After = clean, eco-friendly, premium packaging

Example CTA:

Upgrade your packaging. Upgrade your brand.

## Pattern 4: Villain Reveal

Original structure:

- Audience expects one problem
- Real enemy is revealed
- Emotional twist

Earth Relief conversion:

- Real villain is not cost
- Real villain is outdated packaging damaging future trust

Example CTA:

Cheap packaging can cost your brand more than you think.

## Pattern 5: Business Wake-Up Call

Original structure:

- Direct warning
- Harsh truth
- Practical solution

Earth Relief conversion:

- Businesses cannot ignore sustainability expectations forever
- Early movers build stronger brand memory

Example CTA:

Sustainability is not a trend. It is tomorrow's business standard.
